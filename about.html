Global Reach

142 Countries and Counting

Darrell Seale has visited 142 countries — not as a tourist collecting passport stamps, but as someone who consistently engages with the environments, cultures, and communities he encounters. From the Antarctic Peninsula to Central Asia and across the Pacific, his global experience spans every continent and is documented extensively on his Instagram, @nomad.darrell.

This global exposure informs his approach to scuba diving instruction and nonprofit work, particularly in how people adapt, learn, and operate in unfamiliar or high-pressure environments. His article on what global travel reveals about leadership explores these patterns in greater depth.

Military Service

Foundation of Service: The Air Force Years

Darrell Seale began his professional journey with a full United States Air Force ROTC scholarship, commissioning as an officer after graduating cum laude from Oregon State University with a degree in Industrial and Manufacturing Engineering. During his service, he earned multiple commendations, including the Meritorious Service Medal and Air Force Achievement Medals.

His military experience also resulted in long-term physical injuries, including shoulder damage requiring joint replacement and other service-related conditions recognized by the Department of Veterans Affairs. This lived experience strengthened his long-standing commitment to veteran support and rehabilitation-focused initiatives. Read more about his transition from military service to aerospace leadership.

Aerospace & Defense

Two Decades in International Program Management

After active duty, Seale spent more than twenty years in the aerospace and defense industry, including senior program management roles at Lockheed Martin. In 2014, he relocated to Abu Dhabi to support international aerospace programs and regional partnerships, where he has remained based since.

During his career, he contributed to complex multinational initiatives and served on the Board of Directors of the American Chamber of Commerce Abu Dhabi. His work in this sector developed a disciplined, systems-based approach that continues to influence his scuba instruction and nonprofit leadership. You can follow his ongoing work through his LinkedIn profile.

He also holds a Master of Science in Engineering Management from the University of Massachusetts-Amherst (1998) and completed advanced program management training through the Defense Acquisition University.

Nonprofit Leadership

Patriot Divers and a Lifetime of Civic Engagement

In 2012, Darrell co-founded Patriot Divers, a 501(c)(3) nonprofit that uses scuba diving as a structured tool for recovery, confidence-building, and reintegration for wounded and disabled veterans. The program is built on the principle that underwater environments reduce physical and psychological barriers, allowing participants to rebuild confidence through controlled, skill-based progression.

Seale served as Co-Founder, Vice President, and Instructor, directly contributing to diver training and program development. He also volunteered with Divers for Heroes, supporting similar veteran-focused diving initiatives.

His broader civic engagement includes participation in the Special Olympics World Games Abu Dhabi 2019 and board-level involvement with several nonprofit and community organizations. The full scope of his diving-based work is outlined in how Patriot Divers uses the underwater world to serve veterans.

Scuba & Intellectual Life

Certified Instructor, Mensa Member, Lifelong Student

Since 1999, Darrell Seale has been an active PADI and SDI scuba diving instructor, completing more than 2,500 dives and certifying over 300 students. His instruction emphasizes safety, preparation, and controlled decision-making under variable conditions, shaped by years of real-world diving experience.

He continues to teach in Abu Dhabi while contributing to the broader diving community and mentoring new divers.

His admission to Mensa reflects a continued focus on structured thinking and intellectual curiosity. Combined with extensive global travel and underwater experience, this informs a lifelong learning approach that spans both physical and cognitive exploration.
